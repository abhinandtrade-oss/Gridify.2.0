Rules & Guidelines

Event Dates: 20/02/2026 and 21/02/2026

The Three Lifts

  1. Squat
  2. Bench Press
  3. Deadlift

Note: Every candidate should perform all these 3 lifts mandatory.

Weight Categories

Day 1 - Men (8 weight classes): 59kg, 66kg, 74kg, 83kg, 93kg, 105kg, 120kg, 120+kg.

Day 2 - Women (8 weight classes): 47kg, 52kg, 57kg, 63kg, 72kg, 84+kg.

Eligibility of Participants

  1. Only bonafied students of MGU-recognized universities may participate.
  2. Participants must meet the MGU eligibility rules.
  3. Must be enrolled as a full-time student.
  4. Should not exceed the maximum years of participation as per MGU norms.

Documents Required

Each participant must produce:

  • Valid College/University ID Card
  • Eligibility performa signed by the Principal/Registrar
  • Age & qualification proof (SSLC, Plus Two certificates, Degree Certificates, Aadhar copy etc.)
  • No student is allowed to participate without an accompanying Team Manager/Coach

General Competition Rules

Scoring & Attempts

  • Each athlete is allowed three attempts in each lift.
  • The best successful attempt in each lift is added to form the Total.
  • Team points are awarded based on athlete placements.
  • In case of a tie, the competitor with the lower body weight ranks higher.

Weigh-In

  • Official weigh-in will be 2 hours before the start of each session.
  • Lifters must weigh in within their specific weight category.
  • Only lifters and designated officials are permitted in the weigh-in room.

Conduct on Stage

  1. Lifters must follow referee commands strictly:
    • Squat: "Squat", "Rack"
    • Bench Press: "Start", "Press", "Rack"
    • Deadlift: "Down"
  2. Failure to follow commands results in a "No Lift".
  3. Any misconduct, argument with referees, or unsportsmanlike behavior may lead to disqualification.

Refereeing & Jury

  1. All decisions are taken by a panel of three referees.
  2. A majority decision (2 out of 3 lights) determines lift validity.
  3. A Jury may overrule a referee decision if a rule violation is identified.

Safety & Discipline

  • Spotters/loaders will be provided for all lifts.
  • Lifters must follow safe lifting procedures.
  • Coaches are not allowed to touch the bar during the lift.
  • All team members must maintain discipline inside and outside the venue.

Teams & Logistics

  • Universities may field teams for: Men & Women.
  • Teams must arrange their own: Travel, Accommodation & Meals.

Appeals & Protests

  1. All appeals must be submitted in writing by the Team Manager.
  2. A protest must be filed within 15 minutes of the incident.
  3. Jury decisions are final and binding.
